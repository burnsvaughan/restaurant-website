Welcome to The Cockentrice
Join us for a celebration of charcuterie and meat cookery — melding traditional and modern cooking techniques plucked from the rich heritage of the Southern United States and beyond. Now open in Atlanta’s Krog Street Market.
Cockentrice
Noun: One of the more fanciful and imaginative dishes of the Middle Ages, made most often by sewing a pig and a capon together to create a single creature, a “new” animal that would not only feed the masses, but amuse and amaze them as well.

New American Charcuterie

Delivered by on-site butchers purveying the finest pastured meats, and cheese mongers partnering hand-in-hand with premium local dairies, Chef Kevin Ouzts presents a decisive menu utilizing the preeminent ingredients he’s become known for at The Spotted Trotter.
